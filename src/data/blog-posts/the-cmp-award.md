## How I ended up on the project

In May 2025, a few months into my time at KPMG, I was assigned to a cross-border engagement involving work connected to The Gambia.

At that point, I was still settling into the rhythm of consulting life, so joining a regional project this early felt significant.

I did not fully know what to expect, but I knew it would be a step up in exposure and responsibility.

## Working on the engagement

The project required collaboration across teams and locations, with different stakeholders involved in delivery.

My work involved contributing within a broader team effort where coordination and consistency mattered as much as technical output.

It was a fast-moving environment where priorities evolved and alignment needed to be maintained continuously.

## A moment that stood out — delivery under pressure

What stayed with me most from the engagement was not a single task, but the pace and coordination required to keep everything aligned.

There were moments where clarity had to be built quickly through communication rather than assumption.

It became clear that delivery in consulting is often about staying aligned while things are still moving.

## The CMP Award recognition

After the engagement, the project was later recognized internally within KPMG with the **CMP Award for Outstanding Service in 2025**.

Seeing the project receive that recognition gave me perspective on the kind of standard the team had delivered.

For me personally, it was meaningful to have contributed to work that was acknowledged at that level, especially so early in my career.

## Looking back

The Gambia project remains one of my early defining experiences in consulting.

Not because of its complexity alone, but because it was one of the first times I saw how coordinated team effort translates into recognized delivery impact.

It is an experience I still look back on as part of my early growth in consulting.

## Final thoughts

This project was a reminder that meaningful work does not always come after years of experience.

Sometimes it comes early — and what matters is how you show up, contribute, and grow within it.
