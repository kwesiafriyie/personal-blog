## Sundays became their own thing

If Saturdays are for fufu joints, Sundays somehow developed a completely different personality.

They became burger days.

Not intentionally.

It started with something practical, I was searching for alternative places to work on my personal projects.

Sometimes you just need a different environment.

A place outside your room.
Outside routine.
Somewhere with movement, background noise, and enough energy to help ideas breathe a little.

And somehow, that search led me to Atomic.

---

## The Atomic routine

When I’m free on Sundays, one of my usual stops is Burger King at Atomic.

At first, it was simply a convenient place to sit, think, and work.

Laptop open.
Ideas scattered everywhere.
Occasional people-watching.
And eventually, a burger appearing somewhere in the process.

What surprised me was how quickly the place became less about food and more about atmosphere.

There’s something oddly productive about working around movement and conversation without actually being part of the noise.

---

## Then there is the kenkey spot...

Atomic also introduced me to a kenkey joint that deserves proper recognition.

The only problem?

I have somehow forgotten the name.

A serious journalistic failure on my part.

But the food remains memorable enough that I know I’ll return and when I finally remember or rediscover the name, I’ll update future posts accordingly.

For now, it lives in my mind as *the mysterious Atomic kenkey spot*.

---

## Looking for workspaces, finding rituals

What started as a search for alternate work sites slowly became something else.

A routine.

A way to separate weekdays from weekends.

Sometimes I go to work on side projects.
Sometimes to clear my head.
Sometimes to pretend I’m working while mostly reorganizing ideas and observing people.

And honestly, that balance works.

Not every productive session needs to look serious.

---

## The accidental burger friendship

The funniest part?

I might be developing what can only be described as a *burger bestie*.

Shoutout to Gideon Okantey 😂

What started as a shared space to get ideas is gradually becoming one of those unexpected routines you don’t plan for.

Apparently burgers are excellent networking tools.

Or maybe friendship just forms more easily over fries.

---

## Final thoughts

I used to think productivity only happened in controlled environments.

Now I’m less convinced.

Sometimes ideas move better when you move too.

And sometimes, in the middle of searching for places to build personal projects, you end up finding small rituals, favourite food spots, and unexpectedly good company along the way.

So for now, Sundays remain somewhere between burgers, side projects, and the occasional search for kenkey whose location I know perfectly — but whose name I still owe this blog.
